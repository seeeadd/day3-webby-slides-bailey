Six months in. A hundred strategies saved. And I still had no idea what to actually do next.

Most people don't quit because they "can't do Etsy." They quit because every time they sit down to work… they have to decide from scratch.

Back then, it looked like "trying Etsy." But behind the scenes, it felt more like:

jumping between 20 different "best practices" and never finishing one
second-guessing every listing before I even hit publish
wondering if I'm wasting time on products nobody will buy
bouncing between SEO tips, "post more," trends, AI… and none of it sticks
spending money on courses/tools… then feeling dumb for still being unsure
getting that stomach-drop feeling every time I opened my Etsy dashboard
feeling guilty for being on my laptop instead of being present with my kids
hearing that voice: "Maybe I'm not creative enough. Not techy. Too late for me."

Worst part?

Most Etsy advice quietly assumes you have 4 hours a day to experiment. I didn't. I had real life. Kids. A job. A brain that was already full.

So I did what felt "safe": more. More listings. More videos. More tabs… and still no clear plan.

What I needed wasn't more tactics. It was a simple way to stop guessing and start making the right decisions on purpose.

Because if you've ever sat down to "work on Etsy"… and spent most of the time deciding what to do...

that's the whole problem.

Not effort. Not creativity.

Decisions.
